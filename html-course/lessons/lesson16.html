Lesson 16: HTML Entities & Special Characters

What are HTML Entities?

HTML entities are used to display special characters that are reserved in HTML or not available on your keyboard.

Why Use Entities?
  • Display reserved HTML characters (<, >, &)
  • Show special symbols (©, ®, €, ™)
  • Add mathematical symbols (×, ÷, ≠)
  • Include accented characters (é, ñ, ü)

Entity Syntax

Entities start with & and end with ;

Format: &entity_name; or &#entity_number;

Reserved Characters

These characters have special meaning in HTML and must be escaped:

Character Entity Name Entity Number Description
< &lt; &#60; Less than
> &gt; &#62; Greater than
& &amp; &#38; Ampersand
" &quot; &#34; Quotation mark
' &apos; &#39; Apostrophe

Example

To display: <p>Hello</p>
You write: &lt;p&gt;Hello&lt;/p&gt;

Non-Breaking Space

Use &nbsp; to create a space that won't break into a new line:

10&nbsp;AM (keeps "10" and "AM" together)

Practical Examples

<p>&copy; 2025 My Company &trade;</p>
Displays: © 2025 My Company ™

<p>Price: &euro;50</p>
Displays: Price: €50

<p>5 &times; 3 = 15</p>
Displays: 5 × 3 = 15
